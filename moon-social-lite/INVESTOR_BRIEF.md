# MoOn Investor Brief

## One-line positioning

MoOn is a direct social publishing control plane for teams that have outgrown fragmented schedulers and need one system across content, accounts, approvals and delivery.

## The problem

- Social teams now operate across more networks than legacy schedulers handle well.
- Brand and agency workflows require approvals, account clarity, governance and reusable content systems.
- Generic scheduling tools often depend on reseller layers or fragmented integrations that weaken margin and control.

## The product thesis

MoOn consolidates:

- direct provider connectivity
- one publishing workflow
- multi-network planning
- account readiness visibility
- monetization tied to workflow complexity
- workspace and approval expansion

## Why this can matter

### 1. Integration moat

The core value is not only UI polish. The value is owning the provider layer and exposing it through one operator workflow.

### 2. Expansion revenue

Pricing grows naturally with:

- more channels
- more brands
- more approvals
- more team members
- more governance

### 3. Product depth

The product already presents as one coherent system:

- Landing
- Demo Flow
- Overview
- Integrations
- Billing
- Compose
- Queue
- Workspaces
- Settings

## Why now

- The social stack is becoming more fragmented, not less.
- Teams need multi-network coordination rather than one-channel scheduling.
- Official APIs keep changing, which increases the value of owning the integration layer directly.

## What is real today

- coherent multi-surface product demo
- provider model across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube and additional roadmap networks
- workspace, billing and integration narratives tied together
- presentation controls for a live investor walkthrough

## What is not finished yet

- live OAuth end-to-end
- durable publishing workers
- production analytics ingestion
- full role and permission system

Do not oversell this. Say clearly that the current build is a polished presentation product surface with a path into a production MVP.

## Best answers to hard questions

### What is the moat?

Direct provider connectivity plus one reusable contract across publishing, analytics and approvals.

### Why doesn’t this become a feature?

Because the value compounds when teams centralize accounts, workflows, approvals, media, templates and reporting in one place.

### Why can pricing expand?

Because workflow complexity rises with channels, teams and governance, and MoOn is designed to capture that complexity as product value.

### What is the next milestone?

Convert the current product surface into a production MVP with live OAuth, durable publishing jobs and real analytics ingestion.

## Tomorrow’s goal

Do not try to prove that the backend is fully production-ready.

Do prove that:

- the product category is real
- the workflow is coherent
- the market can expand beyond creator tooling
- the business model is understandable
- the product direction is disciplined
